الحَرَكَات

Short Vowels

Harakat are small marks on Arabic letters that tell you how to pronounce them. In this lesson you'll learn the three most important ones — Fatha, Kasra, and Damma.

03 · Intro (concept)

بَ

Fatha · فَتْحَة

"a" as in cat
Sits above the letter

The Fatha is a small diagonal stroke written above the letter. It gives a short "aa" sound — like the word cat.

كَ · تَ · بَ
Ka · Ta · Ba
وَ · لَ · دَ
Wa · La · Da
ذَ · هَ · بَ
Dha · Ha · Ba

Sukun سُكُون

The Sukun is a small circle written above a letter. It means the letter has no vowel — it's held still and silent.

With vs. Without Sukun

With Sukun
مَكْبَ
mak-ba (held)
Without
كَتَبَ
ka-ta-ba (flowing)
